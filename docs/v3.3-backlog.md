# PyOD v3.3.0 Backlog — Literature Gap List

> Maintained from research feeds. Items listed here are recommendations from
> 2024-2026 OD/AD literature that PyOD does not currently support. Each one
> is a candidate for a future v3.x release.
>
> Initial entries seeded from the research pass at
> `docs/superpowers/research/2026-04-13-od-ad-state-of-art.md`.

## High-priority

1. **Extended Isolation Forest (EIF)**
   - **What:** Hyperplane-based variant of Isolation Forest that resolves the axis-aligned bias.
   - **Why it matters:** NeurIPS 2024 "How Many Do We Need?" identifies EIF as the strongest global-anomaly detector across 52 tabular datasets. PyOD ships IForest and DIF but not EIF specifically.
   - **Source:** NeurIPS 2024 poster 98305 — https://neurips.cc/virtual/2024/poster/98305
   - **Effort estimate:** S
   - **Notes:** Reference implementation widely available; should fit cleanly under `pyod.models.eif` with the standard BaseDetector contract.

2. **Time-series foundation model encoder wrappers (Chronos / MOMENT / TimesFM / Toto)**
   - **What:** Encode series with a frozen TSFM, then run classical OD on the embeddings — analogous to PyOD's existing `EmbeddingOD` for tabular text/image but for time series.
   - **Why it matters:** THEMIS (arxiv:2510.03911) and Datadog's Toto blog (2025) both demonstrate that frozen TSFM encoders + classical OD give SOTA-or-tied results without training a deep TSAD model. Direct extension of PyOD's two-step pattern into the time-series modality.
   - **Source:** arxiv:2510.03911; https://www.datadoghq.com/blog/datadog-time-series-foundation-model/
   - **Effort estimate:** M
   - **Notes:** Should land as a `TimeSeriesEmbeddingOD` wrapper that takes any HuggingFace TSFM checkpoint. The encoder dependency is the trickiest packaging question — likely needs a `pyod[ts-embedding]` extra.

3. **Conformal outlier detection with coverage guarantees**
   - **What:** Conformal-prediction wrapper around any base detector, providing distribution-free coverage guarantees.
   - **Why it matters:** Conformal wrappers are increasingly standard for production OD where calibrated false-positive rates matter (medical, fraud, safety). ICML 2025 paper extends to the contaminated-reference case.
   - **Source:** "Robust Conformal Outlier Detection under Contaminated Reference Data", ICML 2025 (poster 43852) — https://icml.cc/virtual/2025/poster/43852
   - **Effort estimate:** M
   - **Notes:** Could ship as a `ConformalDetector` decorator that wraps any `BaseDetector`. Compatible with the entire existing detector zoo.

4. **AutoAD / automated detector selection as a first-class API**
   - **What:** Per-dataset detector recommendation backed by meta-features and a learned ranker.
   - **Why it matters:** ADEngine is already heading this way, but neither TSB-AutoAD's automated per-dataset detector-selection nor a fully agentic AutoAD pipeline is shipped as a first-class PyOD API yet. Aligns with the "no single model dominates" finding from NLP-ADBench (EMNLP Findings 2025) and TSB-AutoAD (VLDB 2025).
   - **Source:** TSB-AutoAD VLDB 2025 — https://www.vldb.org/pvldb/vol18/p4364-liu.pdf ; AD-AGENT arxiv:2505.12594
   - **Effort estimate:** L
   - **Notes:** This is the spiritual sequel to ADEngine's planner. v3.3.0 could promote `engine.plan()` from heuristic-driven to ranker-driven, with a small bundled meta-model.

## Medium-priority

5. **Deep PU-learning AD for contaminated unlabeled data**
   - **What:** Positive-unlabeled learning AD framework that handles the case where the unlabeled pool itself contains anomalies.
   - **Why it matters:** PyOD has DevNet/XGBOD for labeled-anomaly semi-supervised, but no PU-specific framework. Production datasets almost always have contaminated unlabeled pools.
   - **Source:** arxiv:2405.18929 (2024-05) — https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.18929
   - **Effort estimate:** M

6. **SPADE — semi-supervised AD under distribution mismatch**
   - **What:** Open-sourced semi-supervised detector for handful-of-labels scenarios where labeled and unlabeled data come from different distributions.
   - **Why it matters:** Google open-sourced this in May 2024 specifically for the "I have 5-10 labeled anomalies but my unlabeled pool is from a different distribution" case, which is common in production.
   - **Source:** https://opensource.googleblog.com/2024/05/semi-supervised-anomaly-detection-under-distribution-mismatch.html
   - **Effort estimate:** M
   - **Notes:** Could land in `pyod.models.spade` with the standard `fit(X, y)` signature.

7. **CATCH — channel-aware multivariate TSAD via frequency patching**
   - **What:** Patch-wise masked attention on frequency spectra for multivariate time-series anomaly detection.
   - **Why it matters:** PyOD's multivariate TSAD coverage (LSTMAD, AnomalyTransformer) does not include the frequency-patching family. ICLR 2025 paper.
   - **Source:** ICLR 2025 — https://openreview.net/forum?id=m08aK3xxdJ
   - **Effort estimate:** M

8. **GGAD / ARC — generative and in-context graph anomaly detection**
   - **What:** Generative outlier-node synthesis for one-class GAD training (GGAD); in-context-learning generalist GAD detector (ARC).
   - **Why it matters:** PyOD's graph detectors (DOMINANT / CoLA / CONAD / AnomalyDAE) are all discriminative and dataset-specific. NeurIPS 2024 introduces both generative and ICL variants that generalize across graphs.
   - **Source:** GGAD NeurIPS 2024; ARC NeurIPS 2024
   - **Effort estimate:** L

9. **UniGAD — multi-level (node + edge + graph) anomaly detection**
   - **What:** Single model that handles node-level, edge-level, and graph-level anomaly detection in one architecture.
   - **Why it matters:** PyOD's graph detectors are node-level only. Edge-level and graph-level AD are structural gaps that show up in fraud (collusion edges) and molecular property OD (graph-level).
   - **Source:** UniGAD NeurIPS 2024 — https://proceedings.neurips.cc/paper_files/paper/2024/file/f57de20ab7bb1540bcac55266ebb5401-Paper-Conference.pdf
   - **Effort estimate:** L

10. **Dynamic / adaptive thresholding layer**
    - **What:** Online threshold recalibration based on rolling score statistics.
    - **Why it matters:** Production blogs (Uber Risk Entity Watch, Datadog Watchdog) all emphasize that fixed thresholds erode under concept drift. PyOD detectors currently expose only static `contamination` / `threshold_` at fit time.
    - **Source:** Uber Risk Entity Watch blog; Datadog Watchdog 2024-2025; multiple 2024-2025 industrial AD reviews
    - **Effort estimate:** S
    - **Notes:** Could ship as an `AdaptiveThreshold` mixin that wraps `BaseDetector.predict()` to recalibrate from a sliding window.

## Low-priority / nice-to-have

11. **Balanced Point Adjustment (BA) and VUS-PR evaluation utilities**
    - **What:** Evaluation-side utilities for fair time-series AD metrics; the classical Point Adjustment protocol inflates F1 from 0.32 to 0.85 on random scores.
    - **Why it matters:** The TSB-AD leaderboard (NeurIPS 2024) makes VUS-PR + BA the recommended evaluation metrics. PyOD's time-series evaluation tooling could ship implementations.
    - **Source:** "Towards Unbiased Evaluation of Time-series Anomaly Detector", arxiv:2409.13053 (NeurIPS 2024)
    - **Effort estimate:** S
    - **Notes:** Pure metric, no model. Drops into `pyod.utils.metrics`.

12. **AnoLLM — dedicated LLM-as-tabular-detector implementation**
    - **What:** An LLM that scores tabular rows directly, following the AnoLLM paper design.
    - **Why it matters:** PyOD's `LLMAD` is a more general LLM detector that covers tabular and text. AnoLLM's architecture is specifically tuned for tabular schema understanding and could outperform on heavy-categorical datasets.
    - **Source:** AnoLLM, ICLR 2025
    - **Effort estimate:** M
    - **Notes:** Partially covered by LLMAD; reconsider after measuring performance gap.

13. **Multi-Resolution Decomposable Diffusion Model for TSAD**
    - **What:** Coarse-to-fine diffusion model for time-series anomaly detection that handles non-stationarity.
    - **Why it matters:** Diffusion-based detectors are an active research direction; PyOD has no diffusion-based time-series detector.
    - **Source:** ICLR 2025
    - **Effort estimate:** L
    - **Notes:** Diffusion detectors are still research-stage; revisit after the area stabilizes.
